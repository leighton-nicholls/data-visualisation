Colour

Inspiration Site

https://www.awwwards.com/sites/panera-land-of-clean (real site at http://paneralandofclean.com/, although the server is unresponsive)

Obviously, this example is way too out of reach for the application I was building and I needed to scale it down. I like how earthy and natural the feel is on it. The fruits in the picture are really realistic (look at that apple!) with the amount of detail exploding on each one. The lighter shadows around the mountain give an exceptionally fresh feel to the overall landscape. I have incorporated this by placing a legend in the top/bottom left corners of the screen (depending on which aspect you are viewing by), and the individual states change colour according to the scale of that legend. For example, a state with a high number of executions in a particular year will evidently show up with a darker red for that particular year, while states with few/no executions have a softer more light orange-y tone. By tweaking the colour value, I am able to add contrast to the eye of the viewer as they traverse through the years. I am giving the map a degree of lightness or darkness by setting the value (tone). This also sets the mood for the user, i.e. if they liked the number of executions or sentences in a year for any state (say Florida), then it stands out.

Line

Inspiration Site

https://witnessco.com/home

I like how each shape (separated by line) on the site (both rectangles and circles) are encapsulating a certain portion of the page. Each shape represents its importance on the page by its size. The animation when switching to another page is nice as well.

On the map are individual lines that are present around the individual states. This provides a focal area of visual interest and allows me to distinguish exactly what I am trying to demonstrate on an individual state level. It works by providing a area of detail for the eye to navigate to to see what the value is for that particular state on a given year. This provides a sense of order and organization on the map and almost, in a way, creates natural boundaries. Organic shapes (i.e. non geometric shapes) are also naturally created as a result of this too.

Gradation

Inspiration Site

https://designmodo.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/02-meagan-fischer-portfolio.jpg (original site at https://owltastic.com/, but the design has changed)

This site brings a nice representation of gradients by the use of multi-color gradients that span across the elements on the page. The contrast in the different colours adds interest but without being overbearing on the user. Notice how the publisher has stuck with three main colour choices; grey, pink-ish and green-ish, each using slightly lighter and darker tones.

This relates similarly to the previous colour design element I mentioned, but I feel as though this is different. On the map is a slider which can be used to progress through 'stages' or 'steps' by traversing through years, essentially creating a gradual change in the map. This helps to create a visual effect of interest between 'cool' and 'hot', i.e. if a particular state has a low number of sentences during 1986-1988, and then suddenly spikes up in 1989 and drops off after that, this can be evidently seen with the colour scaler acting behind the scenes. In a way, this creates a sentence of movement for the user, helping to provide another platform of interest and engagement.
